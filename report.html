My Experience

Over the course of this module, I have learned the basics of web development, including HTML, CSS, and how websites are structured. At the beginning, I found it quite challenging to understand how different parts of a webpage connect together, especially when linking multiple pages. However, as I practiced more, I became more confident in writing code and understanding how everything works together.

One of the main challenges I faced was debugging errors in my code. Sometimes small mistakes, such as missing tags or incorrect file names, would cause the page not to work properly. Although this was frustrating at times, it helped me improve my problem-solving skills and attention to detail. Over time, I became better at identifying and fixing these issues.

In terms of design, I wanted my website to look clean and simple. I chose a neutral colour scheme with dark headers and light backgrounds to make the content easy to read. I used a consistent navigation bar across all pages so users can easily move between sections. I also experimented with layouts, such as using a grid system on the projects page to display content in a structured and visually appealing way.

I was influenced by modern portfolio websites that focus on simplicity and usability. These designs helped me understand the importance of user interface (UI) and how layout, spacing, and colour choices affect the user experience. I aimed to make my site easy to navigate and visually consistent.

Overall, this project has helped me develop both my technical skills and my understanding of web design. While there were some difficulties along the way, I feel more confident in creating websites and look forward to improving my skills further in the future.
